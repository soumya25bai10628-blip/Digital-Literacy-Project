***STAY SAFE ONLINE — PHISHING PREVENTION CHECKLIST for College Students in India***





**SPOTTING PHISHING ATTEMPTS** 



1\. Always check the sender's email address carefully — official emails from your bank or college will never come from gmail.com or random domains like "sbi-support123.com".



2\. Never trust messages that create panic or urgency — phrases like "Your account will be deleted in 24 hours" are classic phishing tricks.



3\. Hover over any link before clicking — check if the actual URL matches the website it claims to be from



4\. Look for spelling mistakes, odd formatting or blurry logos in emails — these are signs of a fake message



5\. When in doubt, go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself — never click links in suspicious emails or SMS



**PROTECTING YOUR ACCOUNTS** 



6\. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all your important accounts— email, college portal, banking apps and social media



7\. Never enter your password or OTP on any website you opened through a link in an email or SMS



8\. Use a different password for every account — if one gets stolen, the rest stay safe



9\. Never share your OTP with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, college or any company



**UPI \& FINANCIAL SAFETY** 



10\. Never click on payment links received through email or SMS from unknown senders — verify directly with the sender through a separate channel



11\. Check your bank account and UPI transaction history regularly so you notice any suspicious activity immediately



**STAYING AWARE** 



12\. Always keep your phone, browser and apps updated — security patches fix known vulnerabilities that phishing attacks exploit



13\. Do not use public Wi-Fi to log into any important account — attackers can intercept your data on open networks



**IF YOU SUSPECT A PHISHING ATTACK** 



14\. Do NOT click anything further — close the tab or message immediately



15\. Report it immediately at: https://cybercrime.gov.in



16\. Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (free, available 24x7)



17\. Change your passwords immediately if you accidentally entered them on a suspicious website



18\. Inform your bank immediately if any financial details were entered
